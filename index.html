01 — Executive Summary

A Platform Built to Answer the Question No One Else Can

The world produces more software every year than it has the capacity to inspect. Criminals exploit this gap — hiding malicious programs inside harmless-looking files and launching attacks that take months to detect. The financial and reputational cost of these attacks has never been higher.

The average cost of a serious breach now exceeds $4.88 million per organization — and in many cases the damage is irreparable.

Griffinix was built to close this gap. It is an intelligent platform that examines software and answers one critical question with clarity and speed:

"What does this software actually do — and is it safe?"

Unlike traditional security tools that only examine the surface of a program or compare it against a list of known threats, Griffinix understands software behavior from the inside out. The result is a platform that replaces days of expensive manual expert analysis with automated insights delivered in minutes — at a fraction of the cost and at a scale no human team can match.

02 — The Platform

Griffinix — What It Is & Why It Matters

Every modern organization depends on software to operate, deliver services, communicate, and store information. Yet software remains a black box. When a company receives a file from a vendor, installs an application, or processes an email attachment, it typically has no reliable way of knowing whether that software is doing exactly what it claims — or secretly doing something else.

This uncertainty is extremely costly. Security teams spend hundreds of hours each month manually reviewing suspicious applications. Skilled analysts are scarce and expensive. The volume of software organizations encounter daily has long surpassed the inspection capacity of any human team — and as AI enables automatic software creation at unprecedented scale, this problem will soon become dramatically worse.

Name & Mission

The name Griffinix is derived from the Griffin — the legendary guardian of power and vigilance. This reflects the platform's core mission: to stand as an intelligent, tireless protector between organizations and the software threats that surround them. Where a human analyst might review one file per day, Griffinix simultaneously reviews hundreds — around the clock, without fatigue or surveillance gaps.

How Griffinix is Delivered

Griffinix is available as an online subscription service. Organizations access it through a web interface or integrate it into their existing systems — no hardware purchases or complex infrastructure installations required. For organizations with strict data security policies that require all analysis to remain within their controlled environment, a fully on-premise deployment option is also available.

SERVICE · 01

Software Intelligence Analyzer

Understanding what a compiled software application does internally has traditionally required a highly skilled specialist who can read machine-level language and reconstruct the program's purpose. Griffinix eliminates this bottleneck entirely.

By receiving any software file — without requiring original design documents or knowledge of its creator — Griffinix systematically examines its internal structure, maps how its components interact, and produces a comprehensive plain-language report explaining what the software was designed to do.

03 — Market Opportunity

Why the Time Is Now

Three powerful forces are converging to create exceptional demand for precisely what Griffinix delivers — and the window to establish market leadership is open now.

Force 1: Volume Beyond Human Capacity

Security researchers identify more than 450,000 new malicious programs every day. No organization, regardless of resources, can manually review even a fraction of this volume. Automation is not a nice-to-have — it is the only viable response. Griffinix provides that automation.

Force 2: Sophistication Outpacing Defenses

Modern attackers design their software to appear harmless until deployed, behave differently when monitored, and change their appearance with each new release or infection — deliberately defeating defenses that rely on recognizing past threats. Behavioral analysis — which focuses on understanding what software actually does rather than what it looks like — is the only defensive approach that remains effective against these techniques. Griffinix delivers behavioral analysis at scale, with full automation.

Force 3: The AI-Generated Software Threat

AI tools can now produce operational software from a simple prompt in seconds. In a short time horizon, this means malicious software can be created in unlimited quantities, at near-zero cost, by anyone with a computer and internet connection — no programming knowledge required. Griffinix's automated, scalable analysis architecture was designed precisely for this future.

05 — Business Model

How Griffinix Generates Revenue

Griffinix is designed to generate revenue from the first day paying customers are acquired, and its business model scales naturally as the customer base grows. Revenue flows from four distinct, complementary channels.

CHANNEL · 01

Subscription Plans

Free Tier: Available to all users with no credit card required. Provides limited analysis capacity sufficient to experience the platform's value — the primary customer acquisition channel.

Professional: For analysts, researchers, and small teams. Greater analysis capacity, comprehensive reports, and full library access. Priced to be accessible to specialists while generating meaningful recurring revenue.

Enterprise: For large organizations requiring dedicated capacity, data isolation, custom configurations, and formal service agreements. Pricing reflects the operational value delivered, with priority support and dedicated account management.

CHANNEL · 02

Pay-Per-Use API Access

Suitable for organizations that prefer to pay only for actual usage, or that integrate Griffinix into automated processing pipelines managing unpredictable volumes. A fixed rate is charged per analysis.

Particularly attractive to technology platforms, app stores, and managed security service providers that need flexible capacity without fixed subscription commitments.

CHANNEL · 03

Intelligence Library Licensing

The behavioral intelligence library generates dedicated revenue through enterprise access agreements. Research institutions, AI development companies, government organizations, and cybersecurity firms pay for access to curated, behaviorally-labeled software collections.

This revenue stream grows automatically as the library expands — creating an accelerating value cycle without proportional cost increases.

CHANNEL · 04

Professional Services

Organizations requiring assistance with integration into existing systems, specialized deployments, or custom configurations engage Griffinix's professional services team. These engagements typically convert into long-term enterprise subscription relationships.

This channel also generates direct consulting revenue from organizations that require tailored security workflows built around Griffinix capabilities.

Why the Business Model is Resilient

Dominant recurring revenue: Subscription and enterprise licensing fees — not one-time sales — form the foundation of the revenue model, improving predictability and reducing dependency on continuous new customer acquisition.

Continuous platform improvement: Every analysis adds value to the intelligence library, making the platform more attractive to new customers. This creates more revenue, which funds further platform development — a compounding competitive advantage that strengthens with scale.

Multiple customer entry points: Individual users, teams, organizations, researchers, and platform integrators each have a pricing model matched to their needs, enabling maximum total addressable market capture.

Low marginal cost: After the platform is established, adding new customers costs very little relative to the revenue they generate, making gross margins highly attractive.

08 — Risk Assessment

Identified Risks & Mitigation Strategies

Griffinix has identified its key operational and market risks and developed specific mitigation strategies for each.

Enterprise Sales Cycles Longer Than Projected
Likely
Mitigation: Multiple revenue streams — individual subscriptions, API access, and library licensing — generate revenue throughout long enterprise sales cycles, reducing dependency on single large deals.
Competitors Adding Similar Features
Possible
Mitigation: The intelligence library becomes more defensible with every analysis performed — a compounding asset that no competitor can replicate overnight. The integration depth of all three services is not easily copied by adding a single feature to an existing product.
Data Sovereignty & Cloud Resistance
Possible
Mitigation: Full on-premise deployment option is available for any organization with strict data governance requirements, removing cloud analysis as a barrier to enterprise adoption.
Operational Cost Pressure at Scale
Possible
Mitigation: Cloud infrastructure scales automatically; enterprise contract pricing is structured to cover infrastructure costs proportionally. Low marginal cost model protects gross margins as user volume grows.
Emerging Threats Developing New Evasion Techniques
Ongoing
Mitigation: Continuous platform updates; the behavioral analysis approach is inherently more resilient against signature-based evasion than pattern-matching tools. Griffinix detects what software does, not just what it looks like.
Attracting & Retaining Key Technical Talent
Possible
Mitigation: Competitive compensation structures; platform architecture designed to minimize single-person dependencies, distributing critical knowledge across the system and team.
